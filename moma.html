Museum of Modern Art (MoMA)

Located in Midtown Manhattan, the museum houses an unparalleled collection spanning painting, sculpture, film, design, and performance.

My First Experience

The first time I visited MoMA was in December 2021. Before going, I had already heard so much about its reputation—it felt like one of those places you have to experience at least once. Walking through the museum, I remember feeling a mix of familiarity and awe. So many works I had only seen in books or online were suddenly right in front of me. It felt less like visiting a museum and more like stepping into a timeline of art history.

Artistic Experience

MoMA’s collection spans a wide spectrum—from early modernism to contemporary experimentation. What stood out to me most was how the museum presents both iconic and challenging works in a way that feels accessible. My favorite piece was by Mark Rothko. His work, with its large fields of color, created a quiet but intense emotional experience. Standing in front of it, I felt completely drawn in—there’s a kind of stillness and depth that’s hard to explain but easy to feel. Unlike more visually complex works, Rothko’s paintings invite you to slow down. The longer you look, the more you notice subtle shifts in color and mood. It becomes less about seeing and more about feeling.

Tips for first time vistor:

MoMA is large and easy to get overwhelmed in. It’s helpful to choose a few key artworks or exhibitions before your visit. Many of the most famous works are on the higher levels, so it’s often best to go up first and work your way down. For example, Van Gogh’s The Starry Night is located on the 5th floor.
