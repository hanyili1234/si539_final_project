The Metropolitan Museum of Art

Located along Fifth Avenue on the eastern edge of Central Park, the museum spans thousands of years of global art and culture.

My First Experience

The first time I visited the Met was in March 2022. What immediately struck me was the sheer scale of the museum—it felt endless. Walking through its halls, I realized this wasn’t a place you could fully “complete” in one visit. Every turn led to a different time period, a different culture, a different story. It felt like traveling across centuries within a single building.

Artistic Experience

What amazed me most about the Met was its incredibly rich and diverse collection. Unlike museums focused on a specific era or style, the Met brings together art from all over the world, across thousands of years. One moment you’re standing in front of ancient sculptures, and the next you’re surrounded by intricate paintings or decorative arts. This constant shift creates a sense of discovery—there’s always something unexpected. Rather than focusing on a single type of experience, the Met offers layers of engagement. You can skim through and absorb the scale, or slow down and dive deep into specific cultures and histories.

Tips for first time vistor:

The museum is huge, so don’t worry if you get a little lost. Pick a couple of exhibitions you really want to see, and for everything else, just go with the flow and enjoy what you find.
